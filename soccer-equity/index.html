USWNT Goes Back-to-Back!

Womens' World Cup | Paris | June 7 - July 7, 2019

On July 7, 2019, the United States Women's National Team defeated the Netherlands to secure the women's World Cup soccer championship. This gave the USWNT back-to-back championships -- the first of its kind in the 28-year FIFA Women's World Cup history, and only the second back-to-back titles in FIFA championships overall (Brazil repeated championships in 1958 and 1962).

This rare and significant accomplishment, however, was overshadowed by controversies about equal pay, traditional gender roles, sportsmanship, and the intersection of politics and sports.

This site explores the reactions to that victory and to the calls for equity pay in soccer.
